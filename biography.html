debut

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, affectionately known as "Mahi" or "Captain Cool," made his international cricket debut on December 23, 2004, in an One Day International (ODI) match against Bangladesh at the Chittagong Divisional Stadium. Following his impressive performances for India A in Kenya, the 23-year-old wicket-keeper-batsman was picked in the national squad by the then captain Sourav Ganguly to solidify the lower-middle order. However, the debut was far from ideal; coming in to bat at number seven, Dhoni was run out for a golden duck (0 runs) on the very first ball he faced, attempting a quick single off Bangladesh bowler Mohammad Rafique. Despite this disappointing start, India won that particular match by 11 runs. The Bangladesh series overall did not yield high scores, but it marked the beginning of a legendary career, with Dhoni quickly shaking off the shaky start to become the greatest finisher in modern-day limited-overs cricket. Interestingly, the same match also featured the debut of Joginder Sharma, who would later bowl the final over under Dhoni's captaincy in the 2007 T20 World Cup final.

highlights

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, affectionately known as "Mahi" and "Captain Cool," is widely regarded as one of the most successful and influential cricketers in Indian history, known for his calm demeanor under pressure and exceptional finishing ability. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Bihar (now Jharkhand), he transitioned from a school football goalkeeper to a cricketing icon, making his international debut in December 2004. Under his captaincy (2007–2017), India achieved unprecedented success, winning the inaugural 2007 ICC T20 World Cup, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup—where he famously hit the winning six—and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, making him the only captain to win all three major ICC trophies. Dhoni's career is marked by stellar achievements, including over 10,000 ODI runs, a 183* score (highest by a wicketkeeper), and over 100 stumpings in ODIs, setting a standard for wicketkeeper-batsmen globally. As a leader, he took India to the No. 1 spot in Test rankings in 2009. Beyond international cricket, he is a talismanic leader for the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) in the IPL, guiding them to five titles. Renowned for his "helicopter shot" and calculated approach, he retired from international cricket on August 15, 2020, but continues to be a respected, iconic figure in Indian sports, having been honored with the Padma Bhushan, Padma Shri, and Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna.

marriage life

MS Dhoni married Sakshi Singh Rawat, a childhood friend and hotel management graduate, on July 4, 2010, in a private ceremony in Dehradun. The couple, whose fathers previously worked together at MECON in Ranchi, reconnected in 2007 at the Taj Bengal in Kolkata, where Sakshi was interning. Dhoni, known as a very private person, has often credited Sakshi as his "Lady Luck" and a calming influence who brought balance to his life amidst the high-pressure demands of international cricket. Their family expanded on February 6, 2015, with the birth of their daughter, Ziva Dhoni, which occurred just before the 2015 Cricket World Cup. Sakshi is often spotted cheering for Dhoni from the stands during matches and is actively involved in managing their business ventures, including "Dhoni Entertainment". Despite his global fame, the couple maintains a grounded and simple life, often spending time at their farmhouse in Ranchi. Their relationship, admired for its mutual support and simplicity, was also depicted in the 2016 biopic M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story.
